project
      overview
Apocile is a graphic novel that immerses readers in a dystopian, cyberpunk-inspired world frozen by systemic oppression. The narrative unfolds across a frozen, post-apocalyptic world ruled by corporate megastructures. One day, the EGG, a machine from the future world that have collasped drops on the Earth in the past times, carrying death notes and warning messages for the previous Earth citizens. The story explores themes of systemic oppression, surveillance, and the fight for liberation through the eyes of scrappy survivors and unlikely heroes. This graphic novel is a combination of 9 chapters and 9 items from the EGG.
On Earth, people were told that hard work and talent could lift anyone to success under the neoliberal system. The system was fair. That social mobility was real. But it was all a lie. What truly determined your place in society wasn’t your effort or your ability—it was your genetics. Those born into the upper class weren’t there because of merit or how hard they worked; they were placed there because of controlled birth programs. For generations, the wealthiest and most powerful ensured that only those with what they considered ‘superior genes’ inherited privilege. Meanwhile, those with ‘inferior’ genes were systematically relegated to the lower class, their potential dismissed before they were even born.

Working Log 12872
In a world where emotions are monitored and success is genetically predetermined, Working Log 12872 follows a scientist who uncovers the truth behind a rigged system. Through an intimate audio-visual diary, viewers witness the transformation of a surveillance device—AR glasses—into a weapon of resistance. Stark black-and-white scenes shift into vivid color as hidden networks of control and emotional exploitation are revealed. This piece blends glitch aesthetics and narration to expose the lies of meritocracy and ignite collective awakening.
